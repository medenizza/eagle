The Problem

Ensuring businesses don't have operational breakdowns

Saving businesses from the costs of operational breakdowns

Breakdowns translate to lost money:

01

Direct Costs

The breakdown itself creates a financial burden.

Repairs, replacements, and parts all come with a price tag. Additionally, any spills or contamination require cleanup.
02

Lost Production

With equipment down, production halts.

This translates directly to lost revenue, as the company can't manufacture or process products at their normal rate.
03

Labor Costs

Fixing the problem isn't free.

Technicians need to be paid for their time during repairs & cleanup.
04

Hidden Costs

Beyond immediate expenses, hidden costs exist.

Rushed production upon restart impacts attention & quality. Expedited shipping or overtime pay for emergency repairs add up.
05

Reduced Capacity

The breakdown reduces capacity.

Even after repairs, it takes time to get things back to full speed, impacting future output and revenue potential.

How much breakdowns cost businesses varies based on several factors:

01

Industry

Some industries, like manufacturing, rely heavily on complex equipment and face potentially higher breakdown costs.

02

Equipment

The criticality and cost of equipment play a role. A breakdown on a single, specialized machine can be far more disruptive.

03

Practices

Companies with proactive maintenance programs tend to experience fewer breakdowns and lower associated costs.

Low-impact industries with good maintenance: 2-5% of operational expenses

High-impact industries with complex equipment: 10-20% of operational expenses

By implementing technological strategies, businesses can significantly reduce these percentages.
